Mahatma Gandhi

lead the successful campaign for India's independence from British rule.

Timeline of Mahatma Gandhi's life:

  • 1869 - Birth at Porbunder in Gujarat (Sudamapuri, Kathiawad)
  • 1888 - Left for London to study Law
  • 1891 - Passed the Law examination
  • 1891 - Called to the British Bar and enrolled in the High Court
  • 1891 - Applied for enrollment in the Bombay High Court
  • 1892 - Came to Bombay to start practice in the High Court as Barrister
  • , 1893 - Representing a Porbunder firm set sail for South Africa
  • 1893 - At Pietermaritzberg station Gandhiji was ordered to go into the van compartment of the train although he held a first class ticket. On his refusal, a constable was brought and he was forcibly ejected, his bundles pitched out after him. He was left to shiver in the waiting room all night.
  • 1894 - Founded Natal Indian Congress to fight colour prejudice
  • 1894 - Admitted to Natal Supreme Court despite opposition by Natal Law Society
  • 1899 - Out break of Boer War and Gandhiji joined Ambulance Corps
  • 1901 - Moved a resolution on South Africa at Calcutta Congress session.
  • 1902 - At the growing pressure from Indians in South Africa, returned to South Africa
  • 1903 - Founded Transvaal British India Association
  • 1906 - Started Passive Resistance Movement
  • 1909 - Wrote ‘Hind Swaraj’ in Gujarati on board S.S.Kildonan Castle on the way to South Africa from London
  • 1915 - Founded Satyagraha Ashram at Kochrab, Ahemadabad
  • 1917 - Champaran Satyagraha
  • 1919 - Massacre at mass meeting at Jallianwala Bagh, Amritsar, Gandhiji implored people to be calm
  • 1920-21 - Khilafat and non-Co-operation Movement
  • 1922 - Chauri Chaura incident and withdrawal of Non Co-operation Movement
  • 1924 - Started 21 days fast for Hindu-Muslim unity
  • 1928 - Attended Calcutta Congress where a draft Constitution of India was adopted on 31 December, 1928
  • 1929 - Gandhiji’s resolution on Complete Independence was adopted at open session of Lahore Congress along with immediate boycott of legislatures.
  • 1930 - All India Congress Committee adopted Civil Disobedience programme
  • 1931 - Gandhi-Irwin Pact was announced
  • 1931 - Decided to restart Non Co-operation Movement
  • 1933 - Individual Civil Disobedience started
  • 1934 - Declared his intention to retire from Congress
  • 1936 - Founded Sevagram Ashram at Wardha
  • 1937 - Wardha Scheme of Education
  • 1940 - Started Anti-war individual Satyagraha with Vinoba as the first Satyagrahi
  • 1942 - Addressed All India Congress Committee of Bombay and Quit India resolution was passed.
  • 1948 - ‘I do not with to live if peace is not established in India and Pakistan’
  • 1948 - Broke fast by taking orange juice from Maulana Azad
  • 1948 - Wrote ‘Congress Position’; suggesting that Congress should cease as political body and should devote to people’s service. Also attended annual celebration at Bakhtiar Chisti’s Dargah.
  • 1948 - The day of martyrdom, was shot dead while on his way to evening prayer ground at Birla House (now Gandhi Smriti) by Nathuram Vinayak Godse.
